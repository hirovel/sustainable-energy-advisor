## Vision Worksheet

Help the user identify their core values and construct a vision statement.

### Step 1: Identify Core Values (pick 5)

From the book's deep values inventory: integrity, generosity, courage, humility, compassion, loyalty, perseverance, excellence, family, health, respect, kindness, creativity, service, growth, honesty, gratitude, discipline.

### Step 2: Life Lessons (3 questions)

1. If this were the end of your life, what are the 3 most important lessons you've learned? Why?
2. Think of someone you deeply respect. What 3 qualities do you most admire in them?
3. What does the best version of yourself look like?

### Step 3: Vision Statement

Write in present tense. Combine values with concrete behaviors:

```
My personal vision (reflecting my values):
[Present-tense statement of how you want to live, grounded in your values]

My work vision (reflecting my values):
[Present-tense statement of how you want to work, grounded in your values]
```

Example (from the book's Roger case):
> "My wife and children are the most important things in my life. When we are together, I commit to giving them all my energy and attention. To do this, I must take care of my body. At work, I hold myself to high standards. As a leader, I demonstrate my values — kindness, caring, and integrity. Whatever I do, I do wholeheartedly."

## Obstacle Worksheet

Map each performance barrier to an energy dimension and a target muscle.

| Barrier (symptom) | Energy dimension | Target muscle | Values motivation |
|-------------------|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Low energy, fatigue | Physical | Endurance, strength | Health, family |
| Irritability, impatience | Emotional | Patience, empathy | Respect, kindness |
| Poor focus, distraction | Mental | Concentration, time management | Excellence, growth |
| Negativity, pessimism | Mental | Realistic optimism | Integrity, courage |
| Lack of deep relationships | Emotional | Intimacy, listening | Family, compassion |
| Loss of passion, meaning | Purpose | Commitment, engagement | Values alignment |

## Ritual Design Template

Adapt these fields to the situation. A short conversation may only need: name, trigger, behavior, tracking.

```markdown
**Ritual Name:** [short, descriptive — e.g., "The 7:00 Door Close"]

**Target Muscle:** [which capacity — patience, concentration, endurance...]

**Performance Barrier:** [the specific symptom]

**Values Motivation:** [which core value drives this]

**Desired Outcome:** [what success looks like]

**Trigger:** [exact time, place, preceding event]

**Behavior:** [step-by-step actions]
1. [first action]
2. [second action]
3. ...

**Duration:** [how long]

**Recovery Element:** [how energy restores]

**Tracking:** [simple yes/no or 1-5 scale]

**Restart Rule:** [if missed: forgive, don't double-up, just do next one]

**One-Time Setup:** [anything to buy, schedule, or arrange once]
```

## The 30-60 Day Cycle

- **Days 1-7:** Conscious effort. Expect discomfort and resistance. Track daily.
- **Days 8-21:** Behavior starts feeling natural. Cravings for old patterns may surge. Stay consistent.
- **Days 22-30:** Ritual begins to feel automatic. Missing it creates mild discomfort — a good sign.
- **Days 31-60:** Consolidation. Introduce ONE additional ritual only after the first is stable.

## Common Ritual Patterns

These are starting points — adapt to the person's actual life.

### Physical Energy
- Morning fuel: protein-rich breakfast within 30 min of waking
- 90-minute work blocks: work 90 min, break 10-15 min
- Midday movement: walk, stairs, or gym at fixed time
- Sleep wind-down: fixed bedtime ritual (tea, journal, read, lights out)

### Emotional Energy
- Transition ritual: park stop, walk, or music between work and home
- Connection time: weekly date night, daily check-in with spouse/child
- Joy activity: dance, gardening, painting — scheduled, not "when I have time"
- Gratitude practice: morning or evening review of 3 things you're grateful for

### Mental Energy
- Protected deep-work hour: first 60-90 min, no email/phone
- Threat reframing: morning journal — list fears, reframe as challenges
- Creativity incubation: scheduled empty time (walk, shower, sit) for right-brain work
- Learning habit: daily 20-min skill practice or reading

### Purpose Energy
- Values review: morning reading of personal vision statement
- Service commitment: weekly volunteer or mentoring
- Honesty check: end-of-day review — any exaggerations today?
- Commitment audit: before saying yes, ask "Does this align with my values? Can I deliver?"
