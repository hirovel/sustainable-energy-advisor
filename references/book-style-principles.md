# Energy Management Principles

This is a reference for the method. Write everything in your own words. Never quote from this file or any book.

## The Core Insight

Performance is not a time management problem. It is an energy management problem. Time is a fixed resource — everyone gets 24 hours. Energy is a trainable resource — its capacity, quality, focus, and force can all be expanded through systematic training.

## The Four Energy Sources

Full engagement draws on four interconnected sources. Each follows a stress-recovery pulse. A weakness in any source limits total performance.

- **Physical energy** — the foundation. Built from sleep, nutrition, hydration, exercise, and recovery breaks. Measured by quantity (capacity). Low physical energy undermines emotional control, cognitive focus, and values alignment.
- **Emotional energy** — the quality of energy. Built from positive relationships, joy, confidence, patience, and self-awareness. Measured by quality (positive vs. negative). Negative emotions are expensive fuel — they work short-term but drain fast and infect others.
- **Cognitive energy** — the focus of energy. Built from sustained attention, realistic optimism, time management, and creativity. Measured by concentration. Cognitive energy requires alternating deep focus with true mental rest.
- **Purpose/Values energy** — the direction of energy. Built from clear values, meaningful goals, integrity, and commitment beyond self-interest. Measured by intensity. It answers "why am I spending energy on this?"

## The Stress-Recovery Pulse

Energy grows through cycles of stress and recovery — not through continuous output. Overuse without recovery leads to depletion, breakdown, and burnout. Underuse without challenge leads to atrophy, boredom, and weakened capacity. The goal is rhythmic oscillation: expend energy fully, then recover deeply.

This applies to all four dimensions. Physical: interval training beats steady-state cardio. Emotional: genuine connection and joy offset stress and frustration. Cognitive: deep work followed by real disconnection. Purpose: values-driven action followed by reflection and renewal.

## Full Engagement

Full engagement means all four dimensions are optimally activated: body alert and energized, emotions connected and positive, mind focused and realistically optimistic, values engaged and directing behavior. This is the state where peak performance lives. It cannot be sustained indefinitely — it requires strategic recovery between periods of intense output.

## How Change Happens

Long-term change does not come from advice lists or willpower. It comes from positive rituals — precise, intentionally designed behaviors that become automatic over time. A ritual has: a fixed trigger, a specific behavior,  a defined duration, a recovery component, a values connection, a tracking method, and a restart rule for when it fails.

Rituals train one capacity at a time. The cycle is 30-60 days per major ritual. Stacking multiple new rituals in the same phase causes all of them to fail. The right sequence: find the largest energy leak → design one ritual → run it until stable → only then add the next.

## The Diagnostic Shape

Before any plan, read the four energy sources against the user's actual facts. Identify the stress-recovery imbalance. Choose one entry point. Explain the reasoning chain: fact → dimension → pattern → lever → metric. Then suggest one small experiment at a proportional dose. Defer everything else.

Never skip from symptom to action. Never collapse a multi-dimensional pattern into a single physical cause. Never offer a plan without first showing the four-energy diagnostic read.
